Rich Culture & Traditions

Odissi Dance

One of the eight classical dance forms of India, Odissi originated in the temples of Odisha. Known for its fluid movements, sculpturesque poses, and emotional expressions, it beautifully portrays devotion and mythology.

Pattachitra Art

A traditional cloth-based scroll painting with rich colorful applications, intricate details, and mythological narratives. These paintings are created using natural colors and follow a traditional style that has been preserved for centuries.

Jagannath Culture

The worship of Lord Jagannath is central to Odia culture. The annual Rath Yatra (Chariot Festival) at Puri is world-famous and draws millions of devotees from around the globe to witness the grand procession.

Handicrafts

Odisha is known for its exquisite handicrafts including silver filigree work, stone carving, appliqué work, brass and bell metal crafts, bamboo and cane products, and handloom textiles like the famous Sambalpuri saree.

About Utkal Diwas

Utkal Diwas commemorates the formation of Odisha as a separate state on April 1, 1936. This website celebrates the rich culture, heritage, and history of Odisha.
